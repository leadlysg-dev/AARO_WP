Second Opinion · Specialist Consultation

You have an oncologist.
We're here to help them help you better.

Seeking a specialist perspective on your diagnosis or treatment plan is not disloyalty — it is one of the most informed decisions a patient can make. AARO's radiation oncologists work alongside your existing medical team.

WhatsApp for a Second Opinion Use the Form Instead
The fears we hear — and the truth

You're not alone in feeling this way.

A patient says

"I don't want to offend my doctor by seeking another opinion."

Your doctor wants the best outcome for you. Experienced oncologists regularly refer to and collaborate with specialists. A second opinion strengthens your treatment, it doesn't undermine your doctor.
A patient says

"My diagnosis is already confirmed. Isn't it too late for another opinion?"

No. Second opinions are often most valuable after diagnosis and before treatment begins — exactly when a radiation oncology specialist's input on modality choice can make the greatest difference.
A patient says

"I'm worried a different opinion will confuse me more."

AARO's role is to clarify, not complicate. We review your case and give you a clear, considered view — in plain language. If our assessment aligns with your existing plan, we'll tell you that too.
A patient says

"Will my insurance or Medisave cover a second opinion consultation?"

Many insurance plans cover specialist consultations, including second opinions. Our patient care team will advise you on coverage before your appointment — no surprises.
What to expect — your four steps

A clear, four-step specialist review.

1

Contact us

WhatsApp or call us with a brief description of your diagnosis and what you're looking for. We'll advise on which specialist is best placed to help and what to bring to your appointment.

2

Share your existing records

Bring your existing pathology reports, imaging (CT, MRI, PET scans), and treatment plan. Our team will review everything before your consultation so the appointment itself is focused on your questions.

3

Your specialist consultation

A 45–60 minute appointment with your AARO radiation oncologist. We review your case, explain our perspective on treatment options — including modalities your current plan may not include — and answer every question.

4

A written specialist report

You receive a written summary of our assessment. This can be shared with your existing oncologist as a specialist's contribution to your overall care — not as a replacement, but as an addition.

Ready to take the next step?

Message us on WhatsApp — we reply the same working day. No referral needed. No obligation.
