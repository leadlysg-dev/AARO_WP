Our positioning

A specialist partner for complex radiation cases.

AARO's radiation oncologists were trained at NCCS and spent years building the advanced radiotherapy programmes that set regional standards. We are not a general oncology service — we are a specialist resource for cases where the precision of SBRT, SRS, or brachytherapy is clinically significant. We work alongside your team. We report back to you. We do not recruit your patients.

When to consider an AARO referral

Cases where specialist precision matters.

SBRT

Oligometastatic disease

Patients with limited (1–5) metastases where ablative SBRT to all sites may improve outcomes — especially lung, liver, spine, and adrenal lesions.

SRS

Brain metastases

Single or limited brain metastases where SRS offers comparable control to whole-brain radiotherapy with significantly less neurocognitive impact.

SBRT / Re-irradiation

Spinal tumours

Primary or metastatic spinal cases — including re-irradiation scenarios where a high-precision boost is needed alongside conventional dose constraints.

Curative SBRT

Early-stage prostate

Low- and intermediate-risk prostate cancer where 5-fraction SBRT is appropriate — including patients unfit for surgery or seeking shorter treatment courses.

Brachytherapy

Gynaecological cases

Cervical and endometrial cases requiring image-guided adaptive brachytherapy as an integral part of curative treatment.

Re-irradiation

Recurrent disease

Recurrent tumours within previously irradiated fields — where dose-painting and adaptive replanning are required to safely retreat.

Referral pathway

How a referral works.

1

Send us the referral

Email patient summary + relevant imaging to referrals@aaro.sg, or use our secure clinician upload link. Our liaison will acknowledge within one working day.

2

Specialist review

The relevant sub-specialist reviews the case. If radiation is not the optimal modality, we'll say so — and we'll suggest what is.

3

Patient consultation

The patient is seen at AARO. We confirm or refine the proposed approach with them, and check that it fits with the rest of their care.

4

Written report back to you

You receive a written treatment summary at completion — and at any decision point along the way. The patient remains within your overall care team.
