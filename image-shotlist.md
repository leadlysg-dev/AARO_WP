## How to use this list

Each section below covers one category of imagery. Within each, every row is a single image slot in the live site. The same filename may appear on multiple pages — those are noted in the *Pages* column. Once a real photograph is taken, name it exactly as listed and drop it into the matching `/assets/images/{category}s/` folder.

Where dimensions show e.g. `1200×1500 (4:5)`, the second number is the aspect ratio — match this exactly when cropping. The HTML page will fill the placeholder with the supplied image automatically.

## Notes for the photographer / picture editor

- **Brand colour grading.** Final images should sit comfortably alongside Navy `#0B2240`, Teal `#0A7E6A`, Gold `#B8882A`, Warm white `#F7F4EF`. Avoid heavily saturated reds, greens or blues that compete with brand colours.
- **Candid > posed.** Across all categories — people doing real work, eye-lines internal to the scene rather than to camera.
- **Patient consent and dignity.** Patient story imagery requires explicit written consent. Treatment-room shots involving patients should be shot with patients who have consented in writing; otherwise use models or anatomical illustrations and label them as such.
- **Lighting consistency.** Doctor portraits in particular should match colour temperature, exposure and backdrop across the seven specialists. Schedule them for the same shoot day if possible.
- **File formats.** JPG for photographic assets. PNG (transparent) or SVG for logos and accreditation marks. Optimise to roughly 200kB per image at 2× retina dimensions before delivery.
- **Rights.** Confirm in writing that all delivered files are licensed for unrestricted use across the AARO website, social channels, print collateral, and clinic interior signage.
