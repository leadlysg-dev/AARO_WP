Cancer types we care for.

Prostate & Genitourinary

Lung & Thoracic

SBRT for early-stage NSCLC, stereotactic radiosurgery for oligometastatic disease. Read our approach →

Brain

SRS and SBRT for primary brain tumours, brain metastases and skull-base lesions. Read our approach →

Spine

Liver

Colorectal

Gastrointestinal

Head & Neck

Frequently asked

Common questions, plainly answered.

If your question isn't here, just ask — WhatsApp us or use the form below. We'll get back to you as quickly as we can.

No. You can contact us directly — by WhatsApp, phone, or the form below. We see patients who come on their own initiative as well as those referred by GPs or other specialists.

Urgent cases are usually seen within a few business days. Standard consultations are typically available within a week. Contact us and we'll advise based on your situation.

All existing medical reports, imaging (CD or digital link if available), pathology results, and your current medication list. If you have a prior treatment summary from another doctor, bring that too. You cannot bring too much — we will review everything.

SBRT and many of AARO's treatments are listed under the Ministry of Health's approved procedures and can be claimed under MediShield Life and Medisave, subject to your plan limits. We work with major Integrated Shield Plans, and our care team will help you understand coverage before treatment begins.

Side effects depend on the treatment type and cancer location. SBRT and SRS are generally very well tolerated — many patients continue working throughout. Hair loss only occurs if the treatment area includes the scalp. Most short-term effects (mild fatigue, localised skin changes) resolve within weeks. Your specialist will explain what to expect for your specific case.

Yes — second opinions are a significant part of what we do. Experienced oncologists regularly encourage their patients to seek specialist input. We will give you our honest assessment, explain it in plain language, and encourage a conversation between your teams. Many patients consult AARO alongside their existing oncologist; others transition their radiation treatment to us.

Yes. AARO treats patients from across the region. We can conduct an initial consultation by video call to assess whether your case is appropriate before you travel — and provide a preliminary assessment after reviewing the imaging and reports you send us in advance.
