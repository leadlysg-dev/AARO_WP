FAQs

Your questions, answered plainly.

The questions our patients actually ask — covering consultations, treatment, costs, second opinions, and travel from overseas. If your question isn't here, WhatsApp us.

About AARO & consultations

Getting started

No. You can contact us directly — by WhatsApp, phone, or the online form. We see patients who come on their own initiative as well as those referred by GPs or other specialists.

For urgent cases, we typically offer appointments within 2–3 business days. Standard consultations are usually available within a week. Contact us and we'll advise based on your situation.

All existing medical reports, imaging (CD or digital link if available), pathology results, and your current medication list. If you have a prior treatment summary from another doctor, bring that too. You cannot bring too much — we will review everything.

AARO is a specialist outpatient clinic, not a hospital. We operate from Novena Medical Centre and our dedicated Centre for Stereotactic Radiosurgery on Adam Road. We focus exclusively on radiation oncology — which means undivided expertise and purpose-built technology for your treatment.

About treatment & costs

Practical questions

SBRT and many of AARO's treatments are listed under the Ministry of Health's approved procedures and can be claimed under MediShield Life and Medisave, subject to your plan limits. Our patient care coordinators will help you understand your coverage before treatment begins.

Yes. We work with most major international private insurers. We will assist with pre-authorisation paperwork and treatment cost estimates before your appointment if required.

It depends on the treatment. SBRT typically requires 3–5 sessions over 1–2 weeks. Conventional radiotherapy courses may be 4–6 weeks of daily sessions. Your specialist will explain the exact protocol for your case at consultation.

Side effects vary significantly by treatment type and cancer location. SBRT and SRS are generally very well tolerated — many patients continue working throughout treatment. Conventional radiation can cause fatigue and localised skin reactions. Your doctor will describe what to realistically expect for your specific protocol.

About second opinions

If you already have an oncologist

Almost certainly not. Experienced oncologists regularly encourage their patients to seek specialist input — it is standard practice in complex cases. If you are concerned, you can simply tell your doctor you are consulting a radiation oncology specialist about the radiation component of your care.

We will give you our honest assessment. If our view differs from your current plan, we will explain why clearly and in plain language — and encourage a conversation between your teams. Having two well-considered perspectives is not a problem; it is an advantage.

Yes. Many patients do transition their radiation treatment to AARO after a second opinion. Others keep AARO as a specialist consultant alongside their existing oncologist. Both arrangements work well — we follow your lead on what feels right.

For international patients

Travelling for treatment

Yes. AARO treats a significant number of patients from Malaysia, Indonesia, the Philippines, and across the region. We can conduct an initial consultation via video call to assess whether your case is suitable before you travel. Our team can also advise on accommodation and logistics.

Yes. Send us your imaging files and reports via our secure upload link and we can provide a preliminary assessment within 3–5 business days. This helps you decide whether it is worth travelling for a full in-person consultation.

Our specialists consult in English, Mandarin, and Bahasa. Malay and Tamil-speaking support staff are also available. For other languages, we can arrange medical interpreters for your consultation.
